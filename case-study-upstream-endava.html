The brief

Build anticipation before the announcement itself.

Upstream Works were about to publicly announce a strategic relationship with Endava — a global technology partner listed on the NYSE. Big news, corporate-sized. What they wanted first was something small, human and shareable — a "day in the life" teaser that would make the announcement land warmer when it came a day or two later. AJH Technology took the brief, scoped it tight, and turned it around fast.

What we delivered

Small piece, sharp craft.

Not every project needs to be a 3-month platform build. Sometimes the right output is a well-directed 50 seconds. Here's what shipped.

01

Concept & narrative

A "day in the life" through-line — travel bag, boarding pass, hotel prep, studio setup, boardroom conversation. Human beats first, corporate news last.

02

Shoot direction

Shot list and beats mapped to the story. Portrait format from the start — designed for LinkedIn feeds, not repurposed from landscape.

03

On-location capture

Travel imagery, hotel prep detail, and behind-the-scenes coverage of a City of London studio session — cameras, mic booms, three principals around a table with the skyline behind.

04

Edit & pace

A tight cut that respects the scroll. Fifty seconds. No dead frames. Music-agnostic bed so the client's socials team could licence audio if needed downstream.

05

Colour & finish

Consistent grade across the beats — warm on travel, natural on the studio boardroom. Portrait crop respected throughout, no letterboxing.

06

Multi-format delivery

Master MP4 for LinkedIn, WhatsApp share and internal comms. Poster frame extracted for static thumbnails and email teasers. Web-embedded on this case study for the record.

Sometimes the corporate news needs a human warm-up. AJH turned a small brief into a piece of content that made the announcement land warmer when it came.
