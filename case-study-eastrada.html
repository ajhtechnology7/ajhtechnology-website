The brief

Sell the contractor, not the cleaning company.

Eastrada Facilities sit in a deliberately overlooked corner of the trade: not domestic cleaners, not soft-services contracts, but a serious commercial outfit with CHAS, Achilles UVDB and Constructionline accreditation, branded vehicles, and the kit to clean a warehouse roof at 45ft+. The job was to build a website that looked and felt like a contractor — not a Yellow Pages cleaning ad — and that turned site visitors into Gary's phone.

What we delivered

End-to-end: from blank page to managed asset.

A full digital build, not a template skin. Concept positioning, visual system, single-page conversion architecture, accreditation-led trust building, lead capture, and a managed-service arrangement so the site keeps evolving as the business grows.

01

Positioning & concept

Brand positioning workshop — "the contractor way" — landed in a one-line idea that drives every section: real teams, real kit, real before-and-after results. No subcontractors, no anonymous crews.

02

Information architecture

Single-page, anchor-navigated site. Seven services, before/after gallery, recent work, coverage map, FAQ, contact. Engineered so every scroll surfaces a new reason to call.

03

Visual identity

Navy #1a3a6e and contractor green #3aa047 palette, Inter display type, "E" tile mark, branded vehicle treatment. Disciplined, premium and unmistakably trade.

04

Conversion-first hero

Hero photograph of a live job — powered access at a warehouse, branded A-frame sign in frame. The first thing a visitor sees is the team they'll meet on site.

05

Accreditation bar

CHAS, Achilles UVDB, Constructionline, HSE Work-at-Height, fully insured — surfaced as a navy band immediately under the hero. The buyer's procurement checklist, ticked, before they scroll.

06

Seven service tiles

Commercial cleaning, gutter clearance, external building cleaning, carpet & floor, roof & moss, warehouse line marking, facilities services. Each with kit tags, scope and a clear contracting story.

07

Before-and-after gallery

The proof section. Real job photography paired before/after with a one-line spec — softwash, hot-water extraction, line marking, roof clean. The thing competitors don't have, made the hero of the page.

08

Two-track lead capture

Free Quote form for buyers who want it in writing. WhatsApp Gary CTA for trades and site managers who want a voice. Both above the fold, both throughout the page.

09

Coverage & FAQ

"Based in Mendlesham. Serving East Anglia." with a written FAQ block for AEO surfacing on ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews and Perplexity. Real questions, direct answers, schema-tagged.

10

Deployment

DNS, hosting, SSL, sitemap, robots, indexing, structured data (LocalBusiness, Service, FAQPage schemas) — all live the day the site went up. No "we'll do that later".

11

Performance & mobile

Mobile-first, sub-2s load on 4G, 100/100 accessibility-friendly markup. Designed for site managers reading the page on a phone in a goods yard.

12

Ongoing management

Monthly content updates, new job photography added to the gallery, accreditation refresh, schema and indexing maintenance — Eastrada don't touch the site, AJH keeps it sharp.

How we ran it

From first call to ongoing managed service.

Eastrada is a working contractor — Gary is on a roof or in a goods yard most days. The engagement was structured so every phase was time-boxed, the client review touch points were short and pointed, and the launch wasn't a finish line.

Concept → Design → Build → Deploy → Manage
Five phases, three weeks to launch, then an ongoing managed-service relationship.
i.

Concept & positioning

One workshop. We mapped Eastrada's real differentiation — directly employed staff, branded fleet, accreditation, photographic proof — into a single-sentence positioning idea: "Cleaning & facilities, done the contractor way." Everything else hangs off that.

  • Competitor scan across Suffolk / Norfolk commercial cleaners
  • Buyer-persona work: site managers, facilities buyers, procurement
  • One-page positioning sheet signed off before any design
ii.

Design & visual system

Disciplined typography, contractor-navy + accent-green palette, "E" tile mark, hero photography direction, A-frame sign motif. Built mobile-first then scaled to desktop. Sketched, prototyped, refined in flat files before a single line of code was written.

  • Brand palette, type system, spacing scale
  • Hero, service-card, before/after, FAQ and contact layouts
  • Branded vehicle photo direction shot list
iii.

Build

Hand-coded HTML and CSS. No bloated CMS, no third-party page builders, no JavaScript framework. Result: fast, predictable, easy for AJH to maintain forever — and zero attack surface for Gary to worry about.

  • Static HTML, vanilla CSS, system fonts where it matters
  • Schema.org LocalBusiness, Service, FAQPage and Review markup
  • Open Graph, Twitter Card, canonical and robots wired correctly
iv.

Deploy

Custom domain on managed hosting with auto-renew SSL, sitemap submitted to Google & Bing the day of launch, Google Business Profile cross-linked, analytics in place. Eastrada was indexed and live in days.

  • DNS, hosting, SSL, sitemap, robots, indexing
  • Form-to-email pipeline routed straight to Gary
  • WhatsApp click-to-chat tied to the brand number
v.

Manage

Site doesn't stand still. AJH adds new before/after jobs as they happen, refreshes accreditation copy, keeps schema and indexing healthy, and ships small UX improvements every month. Eastrada keeps cleaning. AJH keeps the website alive.

  • Monthly retainer covers content, photography integration, SEO & AEO maintenance
  • New case studies and before/after sets added as jobs complete
  • Quarterly performance review with Gary, in plain English

A contractor palette, applied with discipline.

Navy authority, contractor green for energy and trust, white space for the photography to breathe. The "E" tile mark gives the brand a logo that scales from a 16-pixel favicon to a 4-metre van panel without losing its identity.
