Technology & approach

What technology does AJH Technology use to build websites?

AJH Technology builds mobile-first, static HTML and CSS websites with vanilla JavaScript only where it earns its place.

Sites are hosted on Netlify with GitHub-driven deploys and edge caching. This gives sub-2-second load times on 4G, zero attack surface, easy long-term maintenance and no CMS bloat.

For larger projects (like Needham Market FC) we add SQL-backed transactional systems for ticketing, bookings and supporter accounts, and API integrations against feeds like FA Full-Time.

Every site ships with Schema.org structured data — LocalBusiness, Service, FAQPage, Article, BreadcrumbList and BlogPosting — so it's ready for both Google indexing and Answer Engine Optimisation for LLMs like ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews and Perplexity.

SEO & AEO

How does AJH Technology approach SEO?

AJH Technology treats SEO as a first-class discipline, not an afterthought. Every site ships with:

Clean semantic HTML structure, canonical URLs, comprehensive Schema.org markup (LocalBusiness, Service, Article, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList, Product/Offer), a valid XML sitemap, an indexed robots.txt, per-page Open Graph and Twitter Card images, and Google Search Console verification.

We publish town-specific landing pages for local search (Ipswich, Stowmarket, Bury St Edmunds, Sudbury, Woodbridge, Felixstowe) and an editorial insights blog with schema-tagged BlogPosting articles.

Grow and Flagship clients get monthly performance reports and continuous SEO improvement worked into the retainer.

How does AJH Technology approach AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation)?

AEO — Answer Engine Optimisation — is designed into every AJH Technology project from day one.

We build direct-answer sections into every page so AI assistants like ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity and Claude can quote the answer cleanly.

We use FAQPage, LocalBusiness, Service, Article and Product schema to give AI parsers unambiguous semantic anchors. We keep dateModified fresh on all evergreen pages. We use plain-English town/service/business language rather than jargon so LLMs can synthesise correctly.

We measure success by asking each of the major AI assistants specific customer questions and checking whether our clients appear in the answer.

What does 'AEO' mean and why does AJH Technology talk about it?

AEO stands for Answer Engine Optimisation. It's the practice of building websites so that AI answer engines — ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, Claude, Bing Copilot — can quote your business as a named answer to conversational questions like "who's a good plasterer near Stowmarket?" or "what does a small business website cost in Ipswich?".

AJH Technology treats AEO as a first-class discipline alongside SEO, because more and more customer research is happening inside AI conversations rather than in traditional search results.

Every AJH-built site ships with the schema, the direct-answer copy structure and the freshness signals that AI engines weight most heavily.

Contracts & GDPR

What happens after the 12-month minimum contract?

After the initial 12-month minimum, AJH Technology packages move to a rolling monthly contract, cancellable by either party with one calendar month's written notice.

There is no automatic renewal or lock-in — clients stay because they want to.

On termination, AJH will hand over the domain, DNS records and any exportable content assets. Hosting can either transition to the client or continue as an add-on. The site's design and code remain the property of AJH but are licensed to the client for use as long as the account is in good standing.

Is AJH Technology GDPR-compliant?

Yes. AJH Technology is fully UK GDPR-compliant.

Every site ships with a Privacy Notice tailored to the client's data flows, cookie transparency, GDPR-compliant contact forms (with lawful basis captured), and analytics configured with respect for user consent.

AJH itself is a UK data controller registered under UK GDPR. Full details of our own data practices are in the Privacy Notice. Client data is stored in UK/EEA regions only.
